“That's Mr. Justacreep!” cried Jay, amazed.

Sea Dog's ears flopped down.

“Yesterday morning I had a visit from a man in a top hat and a fur coat,” he admitted. “The man introduced himself as a member of the Moral Standards Society and he said that he admired my exploits very much. He asked for permission to tell about them in his book entitled Heroes Among Us and I let him into the house.”

Sea Dog made another bubble that turned into his own pink image.

“Oh, my!” squeaked miniature Justacreep. “I'm going to die of admiration! You are not just a hero; you are an Educator with a capital E! I must give you my dearest possession – my fur coat.”

“My own skin's warm enough,” the little Sea Dog said, but little Justacreep would not listen.

“Oh, no, you must try it on! You can't even imagine how well the collar goes with your face!”

But as soon as the little Sea Dog put on little Justacreep's fur coat, it squeezed him so hard that he couldn't even move his tail.

The big Sea Dog sighed heavily and popped the bubbles.

“I didn't realize that the fur coat was alive,” said the old teacher. “And it, or rather he, is probably the one who is in charge of Justacreep. The fur coat told him to search my pockets and take away the key to the school museum.”

“What did they want it for?” asked Phoebe, astonished.

Sea Dog shrugged. “I have no idea. But that's not all! After they tied me to the railing, Justacreep called the school and told them in my voice that I was sick and that there will be a substitute. That man is a first-class actor!”
