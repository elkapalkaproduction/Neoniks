Jay played for a very long time, until late at night, having overcome many dangers and tricky traps, he completed the last level.

A pink cloud rose above the screen and golden letters shone through it:

Congratulations!

You have won the first prize and can now create a real fox

that will be your faithful friend.

Have a double ball of magic ready and write down this spell. Beware:

These magic words are single-use only; if you make a mistake, they will be lost

FOREVER!

Jay had never been so happy in his life!

Mom had just given him a double ball of magic to finish his homework, but now he did not even think about it. All he wanted to do was to create a real magic fox!

To tell the truth, Jay was afraid that he might not have enough Will Power – not doing homework always had an adverse effect on a kid's magic abilities.

Jay took his Willmeter, a special device to check Will Power, and shoved it under his arm. The results were dreadful – there was just a little Will Power left.

To gain it back, Jay helped Mom in the kitchen, put away his toys, brushed his teeth, and even looked into his sister's room and wished her good night.

“Did the good fairies put a spell on you?” said Phoebe, really surprised. Her brother had never been very polite.

As soon as Mom closed the door to Jay's room, he put on his goggles, got the double ball of magic, and began to chant the spell. “Shalaam-balaam! Abara-kadabara!”

The spell was made up of funny words, quiet whistling, and weird dance moves.

Finally, the ball of magic shone with a lilac light. There was a pop, and a tiny white fox appeared in a cloud of mist, which slowly cleared away. She had blue eyes and a violet gem on her forehead.

“Hi!” she said.

Jay gently picked her up. “Hurray! It worked!”
