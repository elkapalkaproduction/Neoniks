The end-of-school-year activities lasted forever. The kids sang and danced, and the teachers gave out prizes to the best students. Jay got a prize for his presentation on the iron butterflies, but he was so worried about Mystie that he could not even get a proper thank-you speech out.

“I really appreciate it,” he said into the mike and with downcast eyes went back to his seat.

When the principle finally announced summer break, Jay was the first to fly outside and begin circling around the school. But Mr. Justpuss seemed to have vanished.

One by one the kids, teachers, and parents flew away. Desperately, Jay went down to the suspension bridge in front of the teachers’ lounge and banged on the door with his fist.
      The cleaning lady, Mrs. Mantis, opened the door.

“Go home! Your mother must be waiting for you,” she said.

“Mr. Justacreep took my magic fox and I have to get her back,” said Jay stubbornly.

Mrs. Mantis shook her head. “There is no one there. The key’s been turned in. Come back for your toy after summer break.”
