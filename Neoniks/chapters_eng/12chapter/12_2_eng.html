Wanda's eyes lit up. “I'll need a diamond necklace then!” she exclaimed as she pulled Mystie off over her head and threw her on the floor. “I won't have to wear this old skin anymore! It's so cheesy!”

While Justacreep and Wanda discussed their glamorous plans, Jay stuck his hand under the seat and reached Mystie. None of the villains noticed him take the little fox away.

He freed her paws, and Phoebe took the amulet off her neck and handed it to Mystie. “Here, hold on to this tracking device and try to run away as soon as we land. We can use it to find you later.”

Mystie nodded and hid the amulet in her mouth.

Jay touched Phoebe's elbow and pointed to the magic tree. A window in one of the painted houses opened, and a tiny ball of magic flew out of it.

Phoebe picked it up off the floor.

“It's too small,” whispered Jay, aggrieved. “We can't do anything with it.”

“I've got an idea!” said Phoebe.

She took the ball and, making a winged watering can, sent it back to the tree. After the Light of Knowledge was shed on it, its heart beat stronger, and soon its people pulled a double ball of magic outside their house and sent it along the path. Another, smaller one followed.

“It worked!” said Jay, thrilled. “That's enough magic to restore at least one pair of wings!”

“Did you remember the Winged Spell?” Phoebe brightened up.

Jay shook his head. “I never learned it. But it's better to have some magic reserves.”

The submarine flew into the crocorhine cave, twisted through the maze, and stopped.

“Get out!” Justacreep told the kids.

They wanted to take Mystie with them, but Furcoat wouldn't let them. “Keep the fox with you,” he told Wanda. “We'll use it later.”

Justacreep dragged Phoebe and Jay to a deep well and pushed them into it.

“That's your time-out,” giggled Wanda.
