“An excellent specimen!” he said and tucked Mystie into the pocket of his fur coat.

“Give her back!” cried Jay.

But Mr. Justacreep ignored him and simply said, “Class dismissed.”

When he left the classroom, Jay's friends rushed to his side and tried to comfort him.

“The teachers have no right to take our things!” Beppo raged. “It's robbery!”

“Don't worry, he'll give the fox back,” said the girls.

Soon the principal came in and called everyone into the end-of-the-year assembly and concert.

Jay was supposed to sing in the choir, but he couldn't make a sound. He kept looking into the audience and searching for Mr. Justacreep. To Jay's horror, the substitute teacher seemed to have vanished.

The evening came and all the kids flew home, but Jay still sat on a suspension bridge across from the teachers’ lounge.

The cleaning lady, Mrs. Mantis, went by.

“Go home! Your mother must be waiting for you,” she said.

“Mr. Justacreep took my magic fox and I have to get her back,” said Jay stubbornly.

Mrs. Mantis shook her head. “There is no one there. The key's been turned in. Come back for your toy after summer break.”
