“Nothing happened,” mumbled Justacreep. “It’s just the kids fooling around, setting off firecrackers for the Festival. My boss said there is some kind of infection in the greenhouse. I’ve got to check it out, so it doesn’t spread to the big trees. What’s your name, kiddo?”

“Lola,” replied the girl. “I am the Star Fairies’ apprentice. They are guiding the starry flower up and left me to keep an eye on things.”

“Let’s hurry to the greenhouse,” said Justacreep. “I want to get back home in time for the festival concert.”

He was so good at pretending to be a magic tree expert that neither the girl nor her staff suspected anything.

Lola led Justacreep along the path to the greenhouse where young magic trees were growing. The staff flew after them and its frowning old face kept its eyes on Justacreep.

When Lola bent down to pick up a ball of magic from the grass, Justacreep whispered to Wanda, pointing at the staff, “Take care of that thing!”
      They kept going, and Wanda hung back a little to attract the attention of the staff’s young face.

“I think you must be one of the most powerful staffs in the world,” she winked. “Is that right?”

“We’ve got to be, by trade,” said the young man demurely. “After all, we protect the magic plantation.”

“You’re much more than just a plain staff! I’ve always wanted to meet someone like you!”

Wanda flirted shamelessly. She paid compliments, laughed contagiously, and asked for advice on all sorts of things.

Mystie really wanted to yell to the staff, “She’s lying to you!” but Wanda’s strong fingers were still squeezing her jaws.

The staff’s old face wanted to see what was going on, but the young one could not pull his eyes away from the magic wand and refused to turn around.
