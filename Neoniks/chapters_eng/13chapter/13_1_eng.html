CHAPTER 13

THE SPELL

Justacreep brought the submarine to the hidden cave behind the oak gates.

“So, what do we do now?” asked Wanda, bounding outside.

“Bolt the gate and put the tree in the middle of the cave,” directed Furcoat.

“Are we going to grow magic?” Wanda hopped up and down impatiently. “Is there enough room for it here?”

Furcoat paid no attention to her jabber. His eyes were shining but his voice was quiet and focused.

“Draw a star on the floor and put a circle around it,” Furcoat commanded Justacreep. “Now take the fox in your left hand and Wanda in your right.”

“Just don't wave me around too much,” grumbled Wanda. “There's so much magic in me, I'm afraid I'll be sick.”

Justacreep untied Mystie's paws and picked her up by the tail. She wanted to yell, but she had the amulet in her mouth, so all she managed was a little squeak.

Furcoat closed his eyes and began chanting in a low, guttural voice. It was such a powerful spell that the walls of the cave trembled and a pillar of fire shot up from the center of the star on the floor.

“Point the magic wand at it!” growled Furcoat, but Justacreep was so stunned by what he saw that he could not move.

Furcoat squeezed his arm and made him raise it. “Skeletus-greedius-minerus-comus!” howled Furcoat.

Thunder roared, and all the magic flowed from the wand toward the center of the star. Without it, Wanda turned into a toilet brush, which the frightened Justacreep threw disgustedly on the floor.

Blue sparks danced within the pillar of fire, and a huge skeleton wearing a miner's hat appeared in it.
