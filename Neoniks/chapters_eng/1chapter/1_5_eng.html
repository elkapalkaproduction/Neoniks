To gain it back, Jay helped Mom in the kitchen, put away his toys, brushed his teeth, and even looked into his sister's room and wished her good night.

“Did the good fairies put a spell on you?” said Phoebe, really surprised. Her brother had never been very polite.

As soon as Mom closed the door to Jay's room, he put on his goggles, got the double ball of magic, and began to chant the spell. “Shalaam-balaam! Abara-kadabara!”

The spell was made up of funny words, quiet whistling, and weird dance moves.

Finally, the ball of magic shone with a lilac light. There was a pop, and a tiny white fox appeared in a cloud of mist, which slowly cleared away. She had blue eyes and a violet gem on her forehead.

“Hi!” she said.

Jay gently picked her up. “Hurray! It worked!”

“Mommy!” Phoebe yelled from her room. “Jay's fooling around with magic again and not letting me go to sleep!”

Mom came back to Jay's room with Phoebe at her heels. “What's going on here? We heard a pop.”
