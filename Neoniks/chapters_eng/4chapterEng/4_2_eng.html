Inside the café, the walls were papered with enlarged pages from children’s books; the chairs looked like powder-boxes with the most delicate powder-puffs for seats, and the transparent tables created a nice illusion – the customers’ legs looked exceptionally slender under them, even those of Madame Hippo.

The massive wooden counter was supported by a live sculpture named Cary. Cake Island was celebrating the Jubilant Moon Festival tonight and Cary had a new hairdo with little carved roses.

Phoebe and Jay noticed right away that there was something wrong – Cary’s face was sad and she had two little wrinkle-cracks between her eyebrows.

“What’s the matter?” asked Phoebe.

Cary pointed to a wide windowsill, which used to house pots of lacegrass. It was a very rare and fussy plant that had to be fed with cookie crumbs, sprayed with perfume, and praised daily.
