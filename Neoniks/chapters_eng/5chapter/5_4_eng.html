“And a first-class thief,” said Jay gloomily. “He stole my magic fox, and Cary's lacegrass, and your key.”

“I think these crooks decided to rob our museum,” said Phoebe. “They couldn't simply get into the school: that's why they made up the story about the sub. Mr. Justacreep left Wanda in the classroom and went to the museum along with Furcoat.”

Sea Dog clutched his head. “Oh, I am such an silly old noodle!”

His Conscience badge started blinking again.

“What do you want?” He was really upset now. “I am not ‘old’? I am not ‘silly’?”

“You are not a noodle,” said Jay and hugged Sea Dog. “You are a nice and kind daydreamer, although you are too trusting and a bit boastful.”

“Thank you,” Sea Dog smiled gratefully. “I have an extra key to the school museum. Should we go there?”

“Definitely!” exclaimed Phoebe. “We should find out why Mr. Justacreep wanted to get into the museum.”

Sea Dog was not one for flying and preferred to use a boat to get to work. He was so good at driving it that he could get there in record time, despite the island's rapids and waterfalls.

Going down-river with the teacher was exciting but a bit scary, just like riding a very wet rollercoaster. Every once in a while Phoebe's and Jay's wings came out on their own – that's how much they wanted to go up and fly away from the boat.
