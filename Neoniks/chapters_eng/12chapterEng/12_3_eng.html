“It worked!” said Jay, thrilled. “That’s enough magic to restore at least one pair of wings!”

“Did you remember the Winged Spell?” Phoebe brightened up.

Jay shook his head. “I never learned it. But it’s better to have some magic reserves.”

The submarine flew into the crocorhine cave, twisted through the maze, and stopped.

“Get out!” Justacreep told the kids.

They wanted to take Mystie with them, but Furcoat wouldn’t let them. “Keep the fox with you,” he told Wanda. “We’ll use it later.”

Justacreep dragged Phoebe and Jay to a deep well and pushed them into it.
      “That’s your time-out,” giggled Wanda.

Justacreep put two fingers in his mouth and whistled; in a minute, a large crocorhine ran up to him.

“Guard them!” commanded Justacreep. “And if they cry too loudly, fill the well with dirt.”

He picked up Wanda, and soon the humming of the submarine receded to nothing.

The well was so dark that Jay and Phoebe could not even see their own hands.

“What if no one ever finds us?” said Jay nervously. “Zoe went for help, but she’s a turtle… who knows when she’ll get to town?”

“Let’s see where Justacreep is taking Mystie,” suggested Phoebe. “She’s much worse off than we are right now.”
