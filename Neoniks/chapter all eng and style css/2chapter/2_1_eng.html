CHAPTER 2

A STRANGE TEACHER

Jay knew that he should not bring a pet to school, but he wanted to show Mystie to his friend Beppo so much that he decided to take her with him anyway.

“What if your teacher finds me?” the fox worried. “Is he strict? What's his name?”

“His name's Sea Dog,” said Jay. “He really likes to talk about his travels and he gets so carried away that he doesn't pay attention to anything that's going on in the classroom. If you're quiet, it'll be all right.”

Jay's school looked like a lot of multi-colored birdhouses connected by suspension bridges and rope ladders.

The classrooms were also unusual; instead of desks they were filled with cozy nests hung from the ceiling.

Beppo met Jay at the classroom door.

“Sea Dog is sick,” he said. “I guess we'll have a substitute today.”

Jay got the willies: what if the new teacher checks their homework and sends him home with a note? How would he explain to Mom about the double ball of magic?

When the bell rang, a gloomy man in a silk top hat walked into the classroom. He was carrying a very unusual magic wand in his hand and, despite the warm weather, was wearing a purple fur coat with a large animal head on its collar.

“My name is Mr. Justacreep,” said the teacher without even saying hello.The kids looked at him curiously, but he only frowned and wrinkled his nose, as if he were disgusted to be in a classroom.“Did you see his magic wand?” whispered Beppo, turning to Jay.It was truly amazing: on one end it had an intricately carved handle and on the other – a fancy cup with a tiny girl who sat in it waist-deep. There was no doubt that it was a wand of the best quality.
