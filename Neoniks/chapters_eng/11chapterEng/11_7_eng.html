A magic tree

     

Half-grown trees, ready to join the others, stood by the greenhouse entrance. Their hearts were tiny and only one or two houses could fit on their bark.

Justacreep sprinkled some demonite powder over one of the trees while Lola wasn’t looking. Right away its leaves turned black, as if burned.

“Gosh! You’ve got plague fever here!” cried Justacreep. “It’s a terrible disease that can spread through the entire plantation! I have to take this specimen to the lab and run some tests.”

“Poor little tree!” gasped Lola, horrified. “Are you sure it can be taken out of the greenhouse?”

“That’s what I am here for,” said Justacreep.

Mystie realized that she did not have a moment to spare. She made herself remember how happily she had played with Jay. It was such a joyful thought that a wave of violet light ran over her fur.

Lola’s mouth fell open when she saw it. “Is this a magic fox?” she cried. “Did you make it yourself?”
