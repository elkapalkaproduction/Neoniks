CHAPTER 2

A STRANGE TEACHER

In the morning, Mystie went prowling around the room looking at the toys and books, while Jay was sitting on his bed with the tip of the willmeter under his tongue. Jay thought it would show that he had no Will Power left because he did not do his homework and lied to Mom. But it was not that bad – after all, he completed a large project and that brought his Will Power up quite a bit.

“Stay here,” Jay told Mystie and went to see what was happening in the house.

Dad was not back yet, Phoebe was still asleep, and Mom already flew to the Museum of Unnatural History where she worked as a senior magic consultant.

The coast to the kitchen was clear but as soon as Jay walked up to the refrigerator he saw Rusty Pomodoro stuck to a fridge magnet by his armor.
     


“I am holding up this door so it does not fall off,” said the little knight. “I did not even follow Milady to work, although she had asked me at some length.”

“Yeah, sure,” grumbled Jay, taking him off the door. “How many times do we have to tell you that it’s not real ice cream in there – it’s just a ghost that Mom is growing?”

“So our ice cream has been enchanted!” cried Rusty and brandished his sword. “I shall find the culprit and destroy him!”

Jay got some yogurt and went back to his room.

“You can’t stay at home,” he told Mystie. “Rusty’s going to look for enemies today and if he finds you he’ll make a scene and will threaten to go on a long quest and die fighting a dragon.”
