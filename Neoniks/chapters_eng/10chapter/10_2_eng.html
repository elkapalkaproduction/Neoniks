Leomen kids drove around town on bikes with sparkling wheels. Instead of trees there were dandelions as tall as palm trees, with filaments that shone in the gloom.

The leoman that saved Phoebe introduced himself. “My name's Ruben, what's yours?”

“I'm Phoebe,” she replied. “I wanted to help my little brother save his fox – she was stolen by an evil teacher. But I fell off the Sky Scooter and got lost. I have to get back to the top; Jay will be worried.”

“You'll have to wait,” said Ruben. “The Star Fairies are going to raise the magic for the Jubilant Moon Festival now, so the exit from the volcano must be completely clear.”

“Where do they raise it from?” asked Phoebe confusedly.

“From the magic tree plantation, which is directly below us, at the bottom of the volcano,” explained Ruben. “That's where the magic we all use is grown. The access to the trees is blocked by a powerful magic field called the Border of Darkness, so that outsiders cannot sneak onto the plantation. If you fly into the magic field, you'll think that you are going down and down forever, while all the time you'd be staying in one place. That's why our island is called Bottomless.”

Phoebe's eyes lit up. “Have you ever been to the plantation yourself?”

The leomen looked at each other and smiled.

“We all work there. Some of us do maintenance, some are agricultural engineers,” answered Ruben. “Today the Border of Darkness will be shut down for a little while so the magic for the fireworks can go through. If you want, you can see how it's raised. It's a very beautiful sight.”
