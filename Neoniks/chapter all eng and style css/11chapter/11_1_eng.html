CHAPTER 11

THE PAINTED PEOPLE

The demonite-covered submarine made a safe landing on the magic plantation.

Justacreep jumped out of the hatch and looked around. “Wow!” he exclaimed, looking up.

Tall trees surrounded him, their bark soft and smooth like skin, and he could see the juice pulsing underneath. The branches looked like vessels and capillaries, and in the middle of each trunk there was a large fruit that resembled a beating heart.

The most amazing thing, though, was the little flat people who looked as if they were painted on the bark. They had painted houses and stores, gardens and workshops, roads and pets; and they were alive and busy, just like a cartoon.

Looking up the trunks at the painted people, Justacreep craned his neck so far back his top hat fell off.

“You are looking at it wrong,” laughed Furcoat. “Look at the roots!”
