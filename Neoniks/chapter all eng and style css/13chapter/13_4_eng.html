Furcoat jumped on the kids, but Jay managed to dodge and pick up the fox off the floor as they flew up to the ceiling. With a deafening roar, Furcoat began hurling pieces of rock at them.

Meanwhile, Justacreep quietly wriggled among the crocorhines, grabbed the magic tree, and started moving it towards the cave entrance.

He almost made it when legs as thick as tree trunks barred his way. Justacreep raised his head and saw three giant policemen.

“Going somewhere with that magic tree?” asked one of them.

“Oh, I'm sorry! I must have lost my way,” mumbled Justacreep, backing away, but the policemen did not let him get far.

Furcoat, realizing that all was lost, soared above their heads in one bound and disappeared in the maze.

Jay, Phoebe, and Mystie came down to the floor.

“Furcoat has the tracking device his chest,” said Mystie. “So we can always tell where he is on the map.”

Jay kissed her forehead. “You are so brave!”

“It's you and Phoebe who are brave!” whispered Mystie. “You saved me!”
