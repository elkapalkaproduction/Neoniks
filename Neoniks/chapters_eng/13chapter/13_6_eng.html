When they came outside they saw many winged Neoniks hurrying down to break the spell on the leomen and the Star Fairies.

Harold zoomed past on the Sky Scooter. He could hardly fit on the narrow seat, but he drove it splendidly. After circling once, he flew up to Phoebe and Jay.

“The good magicians found the Sky Scooter – it was flying above the magic plantation.” Harold pulled a handlebar and rolled over. “Look, I'm a real flying dragon now!”

“And I can't fly at all,” sighed Phoebe. “Mom will be awfully upset when she finds out.”

A giant policewoman was walking by with the magic tree in her arms. All of a sudden, its heart began to beat so loudly that she stopped. “What's the matter with it?” she wondered.

Phoebe and Jay came over to her. A window of the painted house opened, a tiny man looked out and threw a double ball of magic right into Phoebe's hands.

“I think he wants to thank us for saving the tree!” cried Jay delightedly. “It's his home, after all.”

Phoebe bowed to the tiny man. “Thank you! I'll restore my wings as soon as I get home and find the text for the Winged Spell.”
