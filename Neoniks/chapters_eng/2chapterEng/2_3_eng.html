“Neoniks like unusual buildings,” Jay yelled over the roar of the wind in his face. “See that crystal ball down there? That’s Dad’s hospital. And that waterfall a bit further on – that’s really a theatre. On show days they draw the water stream apart like a curtain and there is a stage right behind it.”

Mystie quietly squeaked in delight.

Jay’s school looked like a lot of multi-colored birdhouses connected by suspension bridges and rope ladders.

He flew to his classroom and stopped at the door. The teacher was not in yet – some of the kids were flying around the room, some were walking on the ceiling, and some sat in the suspended nests and, blocking their ears with their hands, repeated the Winged Words.

Jay’s heart missed a beat – what if Sea Dog gave a pop quiz after all?

Jay’s best friend Beppo flew in.

     

Cake Island Magic School

“Class is cancelled!” he announced. “Sea Dog can’t come in today!”

The kids looked at each other and yelled, “Hurray!”
