“Help!” the fox kept crying, but once again, no one heard her tiny voice.

When Justacreep tumbled outside, Phoebe and Jay got in his way.

“Where's Mystie?” Jay demanded.

“I'm here!” she yelled as loud as she could.

The kids grabbed Justacreep by the lapels of his fur coat. “Give Mystie back!”

The animal face on the coat collar turned live and snapped its horrible teeth at them. Justacreep took a demonite rock from his breast pocket and touched first Jay and then his sister with it.

They felt as if they were struck by lightning.

“What did you do?” screamed Jay. “We can't fly now!”

“You shouldn't meddle in things that don't concern you!” smirked Justacreep as he darted away.

Harold came out of the stadium, shaking with cold.

“Justacreep is taking your fox to Bottomless Island!” he said through chattering teeth.

“What can we do?” cried Jay desperately. “We'll never save Mystie without our wings!”

“There is a special spell to restore your wings,” said Harold, “the Winged Spell!”

“But I don't know it!” sobbed Jay. “I was supposed to memorize it yesterday, but I was too lazy!”

“Maybe your sister remembers it?”

But Phoebe shook her head. “We had the Winged Spell two years ago and I don't remember anything. In any case, we would need double balls of magic and we have less than a grain. We'll have to go back home and wait for Mom to come back from work or manage without the wings somehow.”

“We can't wait!” said Jay firmly. “Who knows what these crooks will do to Mystie?”

“Then let's not waste any time,” said Harold. “I'll take you to the boat station; you can go to Bottomless Island from there and save your fox.”
