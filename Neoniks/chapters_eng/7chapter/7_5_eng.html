The dragon turned around and saw Justacreep running towards him; he was waving Wanda as if he was going to put a dreadful spell on him.

Harold ran for it but he got lost in all the intricate stadium passages. Finally he saw a bright light at the end of the hallway. He thought it was an exit to the street and made towards it. There was a roar of the crowd, and quite suddenly Harold flew out on the ice where the fire hockey game was in full swing.

“Watch out!” yelled a huge green dragon in shining armor.

A stream of fire went past Harold's head, and the next moment he turned into an icicle, frozen by the frost-breath of another player.

The referee whistled and there was a great stir in the stands. Justacreep ran up to Harold and snatched the cup that held Mystie.

“Help!” the fox kept crying, but once again, no one heard her tiny voice.

When Justacreep tumbled outside, Phoebe and Jay got in his way.

“Where's Mystie?” Jay demanded.

“I'm here!” she yelled as loud as she could.

The kids grabbed Justacreep by the lapels of his fur coat. “Give Mystie back!”

The animal face on the coat collar turned live and snapped its horrible teeth at them. Justacreep took a demonite rock from his breast pocket and touched first Jay and then his sister with it.

They felt as if they were struck by lightning.

“What did you do?” screamed Jay. “We can't fly now!”

“You shouldn't meddle in things that don't concern you!” smirked Justacreep as he darted away.
