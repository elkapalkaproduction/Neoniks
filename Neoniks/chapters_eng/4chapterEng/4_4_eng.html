“Mr. Justacreep stole my magic fox too,” said Jay. “Phoebe and I want to find him. Do you know where he might’ve gone to?”

“He’s never been here before,” Cary shook her head. “I think he was just passing by, saw the lacegrass in the window, and decided to take it.”

“I wonder what he needs it for?” said Phoebe thoughtfully.

“He must be a maniac who hates beauty,” sighed Cary. “I overheard him yell at his wand that she was unable to do magic, ‘I don’t care about your good looks, you powerless dimwit! They were right to throw you out of the Magic Wand College for academic failure.’ He was very rude to her.”

“Did they say anything about my fox?” asked Jay.

“That’s right,” said Cary, “they mentioned a fox that had passed out.”
     
“Did they hurt her?” cried Jay, shocked.

“I don’t know. I just heard Justacreep say that the experiment with the fox went off without a hitch.”

Jay balled up his fists. “We have to save Mystie! Otherwise these creeps will kill her.”

“Let’s fly over to Sea Dog’s house,” suggested Phoebe. “After all, he should know where his substitute came from!”

“If you find Justacreep, be sure to tell me,” said Cary as they were saying goodbye. “I’ll make him pay for damages – I’ll have him praising my lacegrass for days!”
