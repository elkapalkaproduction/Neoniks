“Can’t you take me with you?” asked the fox.

Jay wished he thought about a hideout for Mystie beforehand – the kids were absolutely not allowed to bring magic creatures to class.

“We have no choice – you’ll have to go to school with me,” he said. “If you stay quiet, it’ll be okay.”

After breakfast, Jay took Mom’s crystals out of his magic wand, put the fox in his breast pocket, and walked out on the air-strip, that is – on the porch.

“So, are you ready to fly?” he asked Mystie and spread out his golden luminous wings.

She peeked out, “How beautiful!”

“You haven’t seen my Dad’s wings,” laughed Jay.

They soared up and he circled around his house that looked like a lantern clinging to a sheer cliff.
