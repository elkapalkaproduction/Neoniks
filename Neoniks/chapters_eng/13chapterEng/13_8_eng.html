Phoebe and Jay came over to her. A window of the painted house opened, a tiny man looked out and threw a double ball of magic right into Phoebe’s hands.

“I think he wants to thank us for saving the tree!” cried Jay delightedly. “It’s his home, after all.”

Phoebe bowed to the tiny man. “Thank you! I’ll restore my wings as soon as I get home and find the text for the Winged Spell.”

But the man waved to her as if he was asking her to wait. The tree’s heart pulsed more evenly, and the kids heard the familiar beat of the Winged Spell in it.

“Buh-toom, duh-doom, buh-boom—”

A light flared behind Phoebe’s back, but instead of the usual wings with dots she had a pair of fantastically beautiful ones with intricate patterns on them.
