“Furcoat, what's going on?” screamed Justacreep. “Why did you call this monster? Why did you waste so much magic on him? We wanted to grow a tree…”

“Shut up and do as you're told,” snapped Furcoat. “Put me on the skeleton!”Justacreep was only too happy to get rid of him.

He watched for the right moment and then put the Miner's arms into the sleeves and flung Furcoat's face over his skull.

Furcoat began to fuse with the skeleton right away. His coat-skirts wrapped themselves around the legs and turned into strong, clawed paws; the sleeves lengthened and flexed their muscles. The face smoothed out, the eyes and mouth came to their right places, and Furcoat sent forth a triumphant roar. Only his chest remained bare, with its yellow ribs visible inside the open collar.

“You lied to us!” babbled Justacreep. “You wanted to get enough magic to make yourself a new body! You didn't need a tree!”

“Of course I needed the tree!” growled Furcoat. “If I have a magic heart, I will be a source of magic!”

He tried to claw the heart off the tree, but Mystie pulled her amulet out of her mouth and threw it in between Furcoat's ribs.
