Mystie finally freed herself from Wanda's fingers. “They stole me!” she yelled. “Don't trust them! They are thieves!”

Lola turned around looking for her staff, but it had disappeared.

Just then the door flew open, and Jay, Phoebe, and Harold ran into the hothouse.

Jay took Mystie away from Wanda. “Don't you dare touch my fox!”

Lola took out a large ball of magic and was about to cast an anti-thief spell, but Justacreep took off Furcoat and managed to cover Jay and Phoebe with it.

“Give us all the magic you've got!” said Furcoat to Lola. “Otherwise I'll smother these brats.”

Harold snorted, trying to breathe fire, but only managed to muster some old soot; he had not used his fire-breath in a very long time.

Wanda, who was glowing with magic, bounded up to him. “I'll kiss you to death!” she hissed, slowly advancing on Harold.

She was small, but she looked so fierce, that Harold choked on the fire he was about to breathe.

Justacreep took away Lola's flask with magic balls and locked her and Harold in a port-a-john.

“We'll take the kids hostage,” Furcoat told them. “If you try to chase us, we'll make them breathe demonite powder and they'll lose their magic abilities forever.”

He ordered Justacreep to take the tray with the magic tree that had the largest heart and three painted houses on its trunk. “We won't be greedy,” he said. “One tree is enough.”

Wanda put Mystie on her neck again, and Furcoat took Phoebe and Jay with them to the submarine. They all climbed into it, and Justacreep started the engine.

“Be quiet!” he told the kids who crouched behind his seat. “And maybe we'll let you go back to your mommy… in about three years.”
