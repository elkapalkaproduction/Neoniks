Looking up the trunks at the painted people, Justacreep craned his neck so far back his top hat fell off.

“You are looking at it wrong,” laughed Furcoat. “Look at the roots!”

Justacreep bent down and gasped when he saw a painted man running along a path with a cart loaded with a tiny ball of magic. Reaching the roots that came above ground, the man dropped his load onto the grass and hurried back.

Justacreep picked up the ball of magic. “Is it real? It’s amazing!”

“Hey, Furcoat, why are they throwing it away?” asked Wanda.

“No one knows,” he answered. “The painted people live in their own world and don’t have a clue that we even exist. We, on the other hand, can neither talk to them, nor send them a message, they can’t see or hear us. Only one thing is clear – they make magic in their workshops and then drop it on the ground.”
