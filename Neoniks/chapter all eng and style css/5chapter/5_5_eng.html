“It looks like Justacreep took the demonite rock,” said Jay and turned to Sea Dog. “What's so special about it?”

“Demonite destroys magic,” replied the teacher. “If a rock like that would touch you or your sister, you'd lose all the spells cast on you. As for magic creatures, they could lose consciousness for a long time.”

“That's why Mystie passed out!” cried Jay. “Mr. Justacreep must have touched her with this thing.”

“He's really cruel to torture your fox!” said Phoebe. “We must save her!”

“If I knew how to help you, I gladly would,” said Sea Dog. “But I have no idea where to begin.”

“Maybe we should call the police and tell them that Mr. Justacreep robbed the school museum?” asked Jay hopefully.

But Sea Dog shook his head. “Demonite has no value – it destroys magic, the most important thing in the Magic World. If I call the police and ask them to bring back demonite, they'll think I am crazy. We keep it here, at the museum, just for display.”

Jay sighed. It was true: calling the police about lost demonite was like complaining about garbage stolen from a trashcan.
