CHAPTER 10

LEOVILLE AND LEOMEN

Phoebe screamed as she fell and desperately tried to grab on to something.

“Help!” she yelled, tumbling through the air.

“Gotcha!” Phoebe heard a bass voice and found herself held by a creature covered in short golden fur. It was a leoman – a man with a lion's head, a thick mane, and a slim, muscular body.

“Are you all right?” he asked Phoebe after setting her down on the ground.

“Yes. Thank you!” she panted.

Other leomen gathered round. They told Phoebe that she was in an underground town called Leoville, set on an interior ledge of Bottomless Island.

Leomen lived in houses that looked like stacks of children's blocks. Sunlight could not reach this far down, so the streets of Leoville were full of lanterns and numerous signs that were ablaze with light and advertised all sorts of nonsense: “This is a Fence” or “Here is a Comfortable Bench.” Phoebe even noticed a sign that said “Nothing Ever Happens Here.”

Leomen kids drove around town on bikes with sparkling wheels. Instead of trees there were dandelions as tall as palm trees, with filaments that shone in the gloom.

The leoman that saved Phoebe introduced himself. “My name's Ruben, what's yours?”

“I'm Phoebe,” she replied. “I wanted to help my little brother save his fox – she was stolen by an evil teacher. But I fell off the Sky Scooter and got lost. I have to get back to the top; Jay will be worried.”
