CHAPTER 12

INSPIRATION AND MAGIC

On the way up, Justacreep and Wanda became wildly happy.

“Now we'll have our own magic tree!” they roared with laughter. “It'll grow big and we'll have as much magic as we like.”

“I'll be a wand-a-girl!” said Wanda. “All the other magic wands will envy me: they'll watch what I wear and what make-up I use… But I'll be so beautiful that they'll whither with jealousy and turn into magic twigs.”

“And I'll be a god,” decided Justacreep. “I want my own temple and followers. I'm not revengeful. If people worship me properly, I'll forget the years of neglect.”

Furcoat just listened to them, smiling enigmatically. “A god of what?” he asked Justacreep.

“I'll pick something easy,” he replied. “I can be a paparazzi god, for example. Let them come to my temple and pray that I send them a celebrity photo.”

“What about you, Furcoat, what are you going to do with your share of magic?” asked Wanda, curious.

“Nothing special,” he said. “I'll get me some gold buttons and silk lining. What else does a plain fur coat dare dream about?”
