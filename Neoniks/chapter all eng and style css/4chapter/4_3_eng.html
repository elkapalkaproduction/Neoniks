“Who could've destroyed it?” Jay gasped.

“A man who came by yesterday,” said Cary, sobbing. “He took me for a still statue and hung his fur coat on my head…”

“Excuse me, but what did this man look like?” interrupted Jay, startled.

“He was wearing a silk top hat and he had a live magic wand.”

“It must have been Mr. Justacreep!” cried Phoebe. “He's a thief!”

Cary nodded her head sadly. “Yes, he is! I didn't want to be impolite, so I didn't say anything and held his fur coat. Justacreep was arguing with his magic wand and I felt so anxious that I began to sing a song under my breath to distract myself. When he left, all our beautiful lacegrass was gone.”

“Mr. Justacreep stole my magic fox too,” said Jay. “Phoebe and I want to find him. Do you know where he might've gone to?”

“He's never been here before,” Cary shook her head. “I think he was just passing by, saw the lacegrass in the window, and decided to take it.”

“I wonder what he needs it for?” said Phoebe thoughtfully.

“He must be a maniac who hates beauty,” sighed Cary. “I overheard him yell at his wand that she was unable to do magic, ‘I don't care about your good looks, you powerless dimwit! They were right to throw you out of the Magic Wand College for academic failure.’ He was very rude to her.”
